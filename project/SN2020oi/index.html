Unveiling the Physics of SN 2020oi in M100

May 20, 2021

SN 2020oi is a type-Ic supernova, the core-collapse explosion of a young star missing hydrogen and helium in its outer envelope. The photometry for SN 2020oi was unusual: rapid follow-up reveals a very small “bump” near day 2. The timescale suggests we’re not seeing shock cooling emission, in which the outer layers of the star are heated by the SN shock and then radiatively cool.

On behalf of the Young Supernova Experiment (YSE), I investigated the properties of the explosion and derived its progenitor system. Our results indicate that SN 2020oi occurred in a binary system, and the unusual early-time data can potentially explained by the ejecta interacting with an aspheric distribution of circumstellar material (potentially from the primary star expanding and filling its Roche-lobe pre-explosion). HST and MUSE data from the SN environment pre-explosion were critical for estimating the properties of the star that exploded as SN 2020oi.
